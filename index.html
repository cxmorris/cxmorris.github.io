About me

I am a freshman intending to study computer science at Harvard University. I am currently involved with Women Engineers Code, Harvard Computer Society, Harvard Women in Computer Science and Harvard Open Data Project. I also worked as a research fellow this past summer in Harvard's Earth and Planetary Sciences department developing graphical models of climate data in Python.

Right now, I am collaborating within a team of 3 to create a new time management app in React. I also am finishing up a to-do list style music practice app in React and command-line Java. Through various school projects, I have created a grade checker in Java and a Tideman voting system algorithm, message encryption program, and photo filtering program in C.

I graduated from Oliver M. Hazen High School in June 2020. While there, I founded a Girls Who Code chapter, captained the school's cross country team for two consective years, served as a section leader in the school band program, and served the community as part of the National Honor Society.

One of my passions is to mentor and helper younger peers. I have previously worked as an intern counselor with Girls Rock Math and currently work with the Pacific Science Center summer camps to educate elementary students in STEM related curriculums. I have also been tutoring privately for about 3 years.
